Oceans, Seas, and Marine Resources

In simple terms, it means taking care of our oceans so they stay clean and full of life for everyone.

Oceans and seas are vital to life on Earth, covering three-quarters of the planet, containing 97% of its water, and hosting 99% of its living space by volume. They regulate the global climate, act as the largest carbon sink, and support human survival through food, medicine, and economic resources.

Key Challenges

  • Marine Pollution:
    • Over 17 million metric tons of waste clogged the oceans in 2021, projected to double or triple by 2040.
    • Plastic pollution is the most harmful, with 89% of plastic litter on the ocean floor coming from single-use items.
    • Marine debris costs an estimated $13 billion annually in clean-up and economic losses.

  • Ocean Acidification: The oceanâ€™s pH has dropped by 30% since pre-industrial times, threatening marine ecosystems and food security.

  • Climate Change: Oceans absorb 23% of annual CO2 emissions and 90% of excess heat in the climate system, leading to marine heatwaves and coral reef destruction.

  • Overfishing and Exploitation: Irresponsible fishing practices and tourism degrade ecosystems and strain marine resources.

Why It Matters?

  • Oceans support nearly 1 million known species, provide 57 million jobs globally, and serve as the primary protein source for over 50% of people in least-developed countries.
  • Coastal ecosystems mitigate storm damage and support 80% of global tourism, generating $134 billion annually.

Solutions

  • International Cooperation: Agreements like the Biodiversity Beyond National Jurisdiction (BBNJ) Agreement (2023) aim to establish legal frameworks for protecting ocean habitats.
  • Local Actions: Reduce plastic use and ocean pollution. Make sustainable choices when purchasing seafood and marine-based products. Consume only what is necessary to limit waste.
  • Policy and Conservation: Increase funding for ocean science and conservation. Implement effective, equitably managed protected areas to preserve biodiversity.
