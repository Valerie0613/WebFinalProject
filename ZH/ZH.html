Zero Hunger

Goal 2: End Hunger by 2030

SDG Goal 2 is all about ending hunger and making sure everyone has enough food by 2030.

ðŸ‘‰ It focuses on:

  • Ending Hunger: Ensuring no one goes hungry and everyone has enough nutritious food to eat.
  • Food Security: Making sure food is available and accessible to all, even during crises.
  • Improved Nutrition: Promoting healthy diets for everyone, especially children.
  • Sustainable Agriculture: Encouraging farming practices that protect the environment and help farmers.

In simple terms, it means making sure no one is hungry and everyone gets healthy food to eat.

Hunger and food insecurity are global challenges that have worsened since 2015, driven by factors such as the pandemic, conflict, climate change, and inequalities. By 2022:

  • 735 million people suffered from chronic hunger (up from 2019).
  • 2.4 billion people faced moderate to severe food insecurity.
  • 148 million children experienced stunted growth, and 45 million children under 5 were affected by wasting.

The world is regressing to hunger levels not seen since 2005, with food prices remaining high in many regions. By 2030, over 600 million people are projected to still face hunger, making the Zero Hunger goal a significant challenge.

Why Hunger Persists?

Key contributors include:

  • Conflict and civil insecurity.
  • Climate shocks reducing food production.
  • Rising costs of living and unequal food access.

Why It Matters?

Hunger hinders sustainable development, affecting health, education, productivity, and equality. Without solving hunger, other global goals such as education and gender equality cannot be achieved.

Solutions

  • Investing in agriculture to reduce poverty, create jobs, and build resilience.
  • Transforming food systems to be inclusive and sustainable.
  • Ensuring social protection and access to safe, nutritious food.
