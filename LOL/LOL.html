Key Challenges

  • Land Degradation:
    • 100 million hectares of healthy land are degraded annually, affecting 1.3 billion people.
    • Globally, 20% of land area is degraded, driving species extinction and intensifying climate change.

  • Deforestation:
    • Agricultural expansion causes 90% of deforestation, with palm oil harvesting accounting for 7% of global deforestation (2000â€“2018).
    • Forest loss threatens livelihoods, increases carbon emissions, and disrupts biodiversity.

  • Biodiversity Loss:
    • 1 million species face extinction within decades, with biodiversity declining faster than ever before.
    • Ecosystem collapse leads to fragile environments less resistant to disruptions.

  • Impact on Health:
    • Unsustainable farming, wildlife exploitation, and the climate crisis increase zoonotic diseases like COVID-19, causing 2 million deaths annually, primarily in low-income countries.
    • Zoonotic disease outbreaks also devastate livestock, perpetuating poverty among small-scale farmers and causing over $100 billion in economic losses in the last two decades.

Why It Matters?

  1. Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services:
    Forests and terrestrial ecosystems support over 80% of the planet's biodiversity, providing essential services like air and water purification, climate regulation, and soil fertility.

  2. Human Dependency and Climate Action:
    Over 1.6 billion people rely directly on forests for their livelihoods. Protecting these ecosystems is crucial for combating climate change, preventing desertification, and ensuring sustainable land use for the well-being of all life on Earth.

Solutions

  • Policy and Conservation:
    • Adopt global frameworks like the Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework, targeting restoration and conservation by 2030 and beyond.
    • Expand and improve protected areas with local community involvement to maintain healthy ecosystems.

  • Sustainable Practices:
    • Recycle, eat sustainably sourced, locally-based diets, and consume only what is needed.
    • Support responsibly managed ecotourism and protect wildlife from disturbance.

  • Ecosystem Management:
    • Promote sustainable forest management and combat desertification to reverse land degradation and support biodiversity.

Conclusion

A thriving terrestrial ecosystem is fundamental to human survival.
